Steering PCB

My first project after joining UBC Supermileage in September 2023. This was a fantastic introduction to PCB design. The PCB itself was actually very simple â€“ it routes three key controls the driver uses through switches or buttons. The start/turbo and the horn controls are push-buttons, while the emergency shutoff is a switch.

Furthermore, the PCB needed to be as large as possible to maximize the spacing between controls while still fitting in the steering handlebars (they look a lot like the joystick in a fighter jet). The components themselves also had to be the right size to fit in the handlebars that were designed in the past.

In the end, the main difficulty was communicating with other members in the team and drivers to properly determine the best set of components to use.

Lastly after everything was designed and ordered, I soldered the components on, tested the connections, and installed it into the two cars!
